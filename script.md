## Single-Speaker Backup

Hi, we are building Traceryx.

AI agents are starting to handle sensitive work: healthcare records, insurance claims, legal documents, and customer support. But when these agents fail, teams debug them with logs that can expose prompts, documents, model outputs, tool payloads, secrets, and customer data.

Traceryx is a private debugging layer for AI agents. Developers add our SDK and get encrypted traces, data-flow visibility, policy checks, and safe traces they can share without exposing private payloads.

Shrey has worked with healthcare agents, where privacy and reliability are critical. Rahul worked at Dell on data storage and confidential-computing systems. I am Tanay, a CMU student building developer-first AI infrastructure.

We are starting with AI startups selling agents to enterprises. Our goal is to make Traceryx the privacy evidence layer for every serious AI agent.

## Name Choice

Recommended name: Traceryx

Why it fits:

- It contains "trace," which connects directly to encrypted traces, safe traces, and data-flow evidence.
- It feels like infrastructure software, not a consumer app.
- It is short enough for a YC video and easier to remember than a long descriptive name.
- It avoids the obvious conflict around "Agent Capsule," which already appears in AI-agent contexts.

Initial availability screen:

- `traceryx.com` returned no Verisign RDAP domain record in the local availability check.
- Web search showed no obvious AI security or developer-tool company using "Traceryx."
- This is not a legal trademark clearance. Before launch, do a proper USPTO and counsel review.
